What is the success rate of hair transplant at Barley? +
Barley maintains an industry-leading 98% success rate with over 1 million procedures performed since 2006. Our patented micro-needle technology ensures exceptionally high follicle survival rates of 95-98%, which is among the highest in the global hair transplant industry.
How long does the hair transplant procedure take? +
Most hair transplant procedures at Barley take between 6-8 hours, depending on the number of grafts being transplanted and the complexity of your case. For smaller sessions (1,000-2,000 grafts), the procedure may be completed in 5-6 hours.
What is the recovery time after hair transplant? +
Most patients are able to return to normal daily activities within 7-10 days after their hair transplant procedure. Initial healing of the donor and recipient areas occurs within the first week.
Is hair transplant painful? +
The hair transplant procedure is performed under local anesthesia, so you won't feel any pain during the surgery itself. You may feel minor pinpricks when the anesthesia is initially injected.
How many grafts do I need? +
The number of grafts required depends on several factors including your hair loss pattern, the density you want to achieve, the quality of your donor hair, and your overall treatment goals.
What is the cost of hair transplant in China? +
Our hair transplant prices in China range from $2,000 to $8,000 USD depending on the technique chosen, the number of grafts required, and the complexity of your case.
Do you offer consultations for international patients? +

What hair transplant techniques do you offer? +
Barley specializes in four advanced hair transplant techniques to meet different patient needs: FUE, FUT, Micro-needle FUE, and No-Shave Hair Transplant.
Can women get hair transplants? +
Absolutely! Barley has extensive experience treating female pattern hair loss and offers specialized techniques designed specifically for women.
What areas can be treated with hair transplant? +
Barley offers comprehensive hair restoration for multiple areas including: hairline restoration, crown coverage, temple reconstruction, eyebrow enhancement, beard transplantation, and more.
How long do transplanted hairs last? +
Transplanted hair is permanent and will continue to grow naturally for your lifetime. The hair follicles used in transplantation are harvested from the donor area which is genetically resistant to DHT.
What medications are provided after surgery? +
We provide comprehensive post-operative medication packages to support your recovery and optimize results. This includes antibiotics, pain relievers, anti-inflammatory medications, and more.
Can I combine hair transplant with other treatments? +
Yes, we often recommend combining hair transplant surgery with complementary treatments for optimal, long-lasting results.
What is the minimum age for hair transplant? +
We generally recommend that patients be at least 25 years old before undergoing hair transplant surgery. This is because hair loss patterns become more predictable and stable after this age.
How do I prepare for hair transplant surgery? +
Proper preparation ensures the best possible results from your hair transplant. Two weeks before surgery: avoid alcohol and smoking, stop taking blood-thinning medications.
What happens if I'm not satisfied with results? +
Patient satisfaction is our absolute top priority at Barley. We maintain transparent communication throughout your journey and set realistic expectations during consultation.
Can I style my hair after transplant? +
Yes! Once your transplanted hair has fully healed and grown in (typically after 12 months), you can style it exactly like your natural hair.
What is micro-needle hair transplant? +
Micro-needle hair transplant is Barley's patented advanced technique that represents the pinnacle of hair restoration technology.

Are there any age restrictions for hair transplantation? +
While most patients are between 25-60, we evaluate each case individually. The key factors are donor hair quality and stability of hair loss pattern.
What is the difference between FUE and FUT? +
FUE extracts individual follicles using micro-punches, leaving tiny scars. FUT removes a strip of scalp, leaving a linear scar but allowing more grafts to be harvested in a single session.
When will I see results from my hair transplant? +
You will begin to see new hair growth at 3-4 months. Full results typically become visible at 9-12 months, with continued improvement up to 18 months.
Is no-shave hair transplant available? +
Yes! Our no-shave technique allows you to keep your existing hair while we perform the transplant - perfect for professionals and women who want discreet treatment.

Are hair transplant results permanent? +
Yes! The transplanted hair is taken from the donor area (back of scalp) which is genetically resistant to DHT, so these hairs will grow permanently and naturally for a lifetime.

What causes hair loss in men and women? +
The most common cause is androgenetic alopecia (genetic hair loss), affecting 50% of men by age 50 and 40% of women by age 50. Other causes include hormonal changes, stress, nutrition deficiencies, medications, and medical conditions.
What is DHT and how does it cause hair loss? +
DHT (dihydrotestosterone) is a hormone derived from testosterone. In genetically susceptible individuals, DHT binds to hair follicle receptors, causing miniaturization - follicles shrink and produce thinner, shorter hairs until they stop producing visible hair entirely.
Can hair loss be reversed without surgery? +
Early-stage hair loss can often be slowed or stabilized with FDA-approved medications like Minoxidil (Rogaine) and Finasteride (Propecia). However, once follicles are completely dormant, only hair transplant surgery can restore natural hair growth permanently.
Why is the back of the scalp used as donor area? +
The occipital (back) and parietal (side) regions of the scalp have hair follicles genetically resistant to DHT. These "permanent" follicles maintain their growth characteristics even when transplanted to other areas of the scalp, ensuring lifelong results.
What is the hair growth cycle and how does it affect transplantation? +
Hair grows in three phases: anagen (active growth, 2-6 years), catagen (transition, 2-3 weeks), and telogen (resting, 2-3 months). Transplanted hairs typically enter a resting phase first, then begin growing naturally at 3-4 months.
Can women experience genetic hair loss too? +
Yes! Female pattern hair loss (FPHL) affects about 40% of women by age 50. It typically presents as diffuse thinning across the top of the scalp rather than complete baldness. Barley offers specialized treatment approaches for women's unique needs.
What is Norwood-Hamilton and Ludwig classification? +
These are standard scales to classify hair loss severity. The Norwood-Hamilton scale (7 stages) is for men, ranging from mild recession to complete baldness. The Ludwig scale (3 stages) is for women, measuring diffuse thinning patterns. Barley doctors use these to recommend appropriate treatments.
Can stress cause hair loss? +
Yes! Severe stress can cause telogen effluvium (temporary shedding) or alopecia areata (autoimmune patchy loss). While stress-related hair loss is often temporary, it can accelerate genetic pattern baldness. Barley's doctors can help identify and address all contributing factors.
What role does nutrition play in hair health? +
Essential nutrients for hair health include biotin, zinc, iron, vitamin D, omega-3 fatty acids, and protein. Deficiencies can contribute to hair loss. Barley's team provides personalized nutrition guidance to support optimal hair growth and transplant results.
Can Barley help with both surgical and non-surgical hair loss solutions? +
Absolutely! Barley provides comprehensive hair restoration including surgical options (FUE, FUT, micro-needle, no-shave) and non-surgical treatments (Minoxidil, Finasteride, PRP therapy, scalp care). Our doctors create personalized plans based on your specific condition and goals.
How should I wash my hair after a transplant at Barley? +
Barley provides detailed washing instructions. For the first week, use gentle patting motions with provided sterile solutions. After 7-10 days, you can resume normal washing with mild shampoo, avoiding direct pressure on grafts. Never scratch or pick at the recipient area.
What products should I use for post-transplant hair care? +
Barley recommends mild, sulfate-free shampoos for the first few months. We may recommend specialized products containing caffeine, biotin, or saw palmetto to support healthy growth. Avoid harsh chemicals, hair dyes, and perms for at least 4 weeks post-procedure.
When can I resume exercise and physical activities? +
Light walking is fine immediately, but avoid strenuous exercise, heavy lifting, and activities that cause sweating for the first 10 days. After 2 weeks, you can gradually resume moderate exercise. Full contact sports or swimming should be avoided for 4 weeks to protect grafts.
How can I protect my scalp from sun exposure after transplant? +
For the first 3 months, avoid direct sun exposure. Wear a loose, breathable hat when outdoors and apply a gentle, high-SPF sunscreen after the scalp has completely healed. UV rays can damage fragile new follicles and cause hyperpigmentation.
Should I continue using Minoxidil or Finasteride after transplant? +
Barley doctors often recommend continuing these medications post-transplant to prevent further loss of your native hair. The transplanted hairs are DHT-resistant, but your existing hair can still thin. We'll create a personalized maintenance plan for optimal long-term results.
What is PRP therapy and how can it help my results? +
Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy uses your own blood's growth factors to stimulate follicle health and accelerate healing. Barley offers PRP as an optional add-on to enhance graft survival, reduce recovery time, and improve the overall density and quality of transplanted hair.
How often should I have follow-up check-ups after my procedure? +
Barley recommends follow-ups at 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, and 12 months. For international patients, we offer virtual follow-ups via video call. We'll monitor your progress, address any concerns, and provide guidance throughout your hair growth journey.
Can I wear hats or hairpieces after the procedure? +
You can wear a loose, soft hat after 3-5 days if needed for modesty or sun protection, but avoid tight-fitting hats or anything that puts pressure on the grafts for the first 2 weeks. Hairpieces or wigs should be avoided for at least 10 days to prevent irritation and displacement.
What should I do if I experience itching or discomfort? +
Mild itching is normal during healing. Never scratch or pick - this can damage grafts. Barley provides anti-itch creams and oral medications if needed. You can also apply cool compresses gently. If discomfort is severe or accompanied by excessive redness or discharge, contact our team immediately.
Does Barley provide long-term hair care guidance and products? +
Absolutely! Barley offers ongoing guidance and recommends professional hair care products specially formulated for post-transplant care and overall scalp health. Our team is always available to answer questions and provide support throughout your entire hair restoration journey, no matter where in the world you are.
