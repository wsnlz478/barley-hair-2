Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions

How does male pattern hair loss differ from female pattern? +
Male pattern baldness typically presents as a receding hairline and crown balding (Norwood scale), while female pattern hair loss usually shows diffuse thinning across the top with frontal preservation (Ludwig scale). Our Chinese medical experts at Barley specialize in gender-specific diagnosis and treatment at all 30+ clinics across China.
Can women use finasteride for hair loss? +
Finasteride is generally not recommended for women, especially those of childbearing age, due to potential risks. Barley's Chinese doctors offer safe, effective alternatives including minoxidil, PRP therapy, and specialized treatments for women at our clinics in Beijing, Shanghai, and other major Chinese cities.
Are hair transplant techniques different for men and women? +
Yes! Men often require higher density in specific areas (hairline, crown), while women typically need diffuse coverage across the top. Barley's Chinese surgical teams use our patented microneedle technology to customize approaches based on gender-specific needs and styling preferences.
How do hormonal factors affect hair loss differently by gender? +
Hormonal changes affect women uniquely - pregnancy, menopause, birth control, and thyroid issues can all impact hair health. Men primarily experience DHT-related pattern baldness. Barley's Chinese medical specialists evaluate hormonal factors as part of personalized treatment plans for all international patients.
Do women typically need fewer grafts than men? +
Not necessarily - it depends on the pattern and extent of loss. While men often need concentrated coverage for bald spots, women may need extensive grafts for diffuse thinning. During your free consultation at any Barley clinic in China, our experts will assess your specific needs and provide a personalized graft plan.
What about styling considerations for female patients? +
Women often have specific hairstyling requirements that we carefully consider. Our no-shave technique is particularly popular with female patients as it allows maintaining existing hairstyles throughout treatment. Barley's Chinese surgical teams work with you to achieve results that complement your preferred style.
Is the recovery process different for men and women? +
The basic healing process is similar, but women may have different aftercare preferences and concerns. Our international patient team provides gender-specific aftercare instructions and 24/7 support. With 30+ clinics across China, you can have follow-ups at the most convenient location during your recovery.
How does the progression of hair loss differ by gender? +
Male pattern baldness often progresses more rapidly with clear patterns, while female pattern thinning tends to develop more gradually and diffusely. Early intervention is key for both genders. Barley's Chinese doctors can assess your individual progression and recommend the optimal timing for treatment.
